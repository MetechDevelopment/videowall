Jean Verheyen

CEO
Nallian

About

Jean Verheyen is founder and CEO of Nallian.
Nallian provides the world’s first open Data Sharing Platform for Business Collaboration. Its mission is to make the world operate as one, one community at a time. Nallian for Air Cargo empowers the different stakeholders in a cargo hub to align cross-company processes by securely sharing and (re-)using data across multiple apps. This generates levels of logistic process integration, efficiency and visibility that historically were only possible within the confines of a single company. Reference implementations include, amongst others, the award-winning BRUcloud.
Previously, Jean was an Executive at Descartes Systems Group. As SVP Business Development, he was responsible for driving Descartes’ global strategic alliances and initiatives in emerging markets and industries.
Jean joined Descartes through the acquisition of Porthus. Before joining Porthus, Verheyen held sales positions at Xerox.
Jean Verheyen holds a Master degree in Physical Education and a Master degree in Applied Economic Sciences from K.U. Leuven, and an Executive Master in Business Administration from the Flanders Business School in Antwerp.

Bert Selis

Cargo & Logistics Manager
Liege Airport

About

Bert Selis graduated from Brussels’ Ehsal Business School with a master’s degree in Business Administration and has spent part of that study at the University of Parma, Italy.

In 2012 he started working at the Cargo Handling department of the former Belgian flag carrier Sabena only months after the bankruptcy of that carrier. At that moment approximately 10 other airlines were served by this company covering a global footprint. Having an international mindset, the cargo handling business (at that time it became Swissport) has quickly taught him how to juggle the interest of many different stakeholders with the aim of creating seamless supply chains.

An inhouse transfer into the Import & Export customs agency and a period at road transport & logistics company H. Essers brought him deeper knowledge of how powerful the sharing of information and knowledge is, and how cargo and logistics flows flouring when these principles are applied throughout the chain.

This period of logistics driven activities was followed by 6 year of international business development for a Pilot Training Institute called Sabena Flight Academy (later on part of the CAE Aviation Training Group). Main accomplishments were the opening up of the Asian market (China & Vietnam) and the opening of training centres in India, Africa, Australia and the establishment of a globally recognised quality training network for future airline pilots.

Nowadays Bert is part of the dynamic Liege Airport in Belgium, one of Europe’s biggest air cargo hubs, and again the integration of all cargo stakeholders is top of mind. An ever-growing no-restrictions cargo airport located at a prime location in Europe combined with an ongoing 470-hectare logistics park development are just some of the tools used to welcome the logistics companies of the present and the future to find their new home at Liege Airport.

 

Steven Verhasselt

VP Commercial
Liege Airport

About

Steven Verhasselt graduated in Brussels, from Ehsal Business School with a Master Degree in Business Administration. He started working for the Regional Development Agency, focused on assisting startup companies with business plans, seed and venture capital. One of the projects he was assigned to lead him into aviation.

Mr. Verhasselt entered the aviation world in 2001 running a project to start up a cargo airline based in Brussels. The project led him to the Middle East and Asia, in search of business partners for the project. Mr. Verhasselt was hired by one of them, and moved to Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, In 2003, when working on a project in Hong Kong, Mr. Verhasselt decided to stay there and start his own company, consulting airports, airlines and aviation related companies willing to develop business in Asia.

Mr. Verhasselt was assigned business development projects for the cargo business at from Asia to Belgium. He was also exploring opportunities for handling companies (Aviapartner for Asia market, HACTL for Africa) and service suppliers (Skytanking, CAE in China and Vietnam). Finally, a number of projects for cargo airlines in Japan (Nippon Cargo Airlines), Malaysia (Air Asia), ASL Aviation (Europe, Asia) and linking up a number of Asian Cargo operators into the African market

Today, Mr. Verhasselt works for Liege Airport,  Belgium’s leading cargo airport. Liege Airport appointed Mr. Verhasselt as business development manager Asia 12 years ago. Meanwhile Mr Verhasselt is vp commercial and responsible for the global business development for Liege Airport, for both cargo and passengers. He is in charge of Liege Airport Consulting, who completed projects in Europe, Turkey, India, China, Congo DRC and Mexico.
